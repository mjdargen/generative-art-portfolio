Overview


Course Description: In this course, we will explore the field of generative art and learn how to create our own generative art. We will seek to apply our fundamental programming knowledge in a new context to create images, animations, music, and more. In addition to developing our own algorithms, we will explore different environments like Processing and leverage out-of-the-box machine learning models to create beautiful and intricate pieces. We will study seminal works of generative art, investigate various data sources, and discuss each other's pieces as we seek to address the ten questions at the core of generative art (McCormack et al).


Learning Outcomes:

  • Use computational thinking to construct algorithms to produce generative art.
  • Showcase the link between art and technology and compare the differences between art and art generated by a computer.
  • Examine the role of creativity, data sources, randomness, iteration, problem-solving in creating art with algorithms.
  • Identify new programming environments and languages, recognize their syntax and control flow, and understand how they can be applied to open-ended, creative projects.
